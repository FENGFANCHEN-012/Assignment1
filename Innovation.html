Innovation Journey

2000 - Haisen Introduces Breakthrough Antiviral Therapy

In 2002, Haisen Pharmaceutical launched its first FDA-approved antiviral drug, HIVBloc-1, targeting HIV treatment. The drug was celebrated for its novel mechanism, significantly reducing viral load in patients. This innovation placed Haisen on the global pharmaceutical map and laid the foundation for further antiviral research.

2008 - Haisen Pioneers a New Class of Diabetes Treatments

Haisen revolutionized diabetes management with GlucoCare XR, a long-acting oral medication for Type 2 diabetes. Approved by regulatory bodies worldwide, the drug utilized cutting-edge SGLT-2 inhibitor technology, reducing blood sugar while lowering cardiovascular risks. This marked a turning point in diabetes care.

2015 - First Gene Therapy Approved for Rare Genetic Disorder

In 2015, Haisen became a leader in genetic medicine by introducing GenCure-1, a gene therapy for Spinal Muscular Atrophy (SMA). This therapy, using viral vector technology, offered a one-time treatment, fundamentally altering the disease‚Äôs progression and improving patients' quality of life. It received orphan drug status and widespread acclaim.

2019 - Development of Precision Oncology Drug

Haisen launched OncoTarget-X in 2019, a precision medicine targeting non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC) with specific genetic mutations. This drug leveraged advanced biomarker identification and became a standard for personalized cancer treatments, improving survival rates for thousands of patients.

2021 - COVID-19 mRNA Vaccine Rollout

Amid the global pandemic, Haisen successfully developed H-COVID-19-Vax, its first mRNA-based vaccine. The vaccine demonstrated over 90% efficacy in preventing severe COVID-19 and played a critical role in worldwide immunization campaigns. It also showcased the company‚Äôs expertise in mRNA technology, opening doors for future applications.
